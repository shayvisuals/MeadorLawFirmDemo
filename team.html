Nicholas K. Meador

Partner

Attorney Nicholas K. Meador was born and raised in St. Louis, where he laid the groundwork for his legal career. An alumnus of Parkway South High School in Manchester, Missouri, he earned his undergraduate degree from Southeast Missouri State University, graduating cum laude. Nicholas completed his legal education at Saint Louis University School of Law in 2015.

During law school, he gained invaluable experience by clerking for prestigious private law firms in St. Louis, where he learned the litigation strategies of some of the city's most successful attorneys.

Nicholas specializes in landlord representation, complex auto collisions, and intricate civil litigation. His comprehensive skill set includes conducting written discovery, drafting motions to dismiss, taking depositions, and preparing expert witnesses. He is well-versed in case strategy, pre-trial briefing, motions in limine, summary judgment motions, and other essential trial preparations.

As a dedicated member of the Missouri Bar Association and the St. Louis County Bar Association, Nicholas is licensed to practice in both Missouri and Federal Courts. His commitment to his clients and his thorough approach to litigation make him a respected advocate in the legal community.

Landlord Representation Auto Collisions Civil Litigation Firm Leadership

Mark Lee Jr.

Attorney

Mark became enthusiastic about criminal law and trial court at a young age when learning about his late grandfather, a prominent St. Louis criminal defense attorney. As a result, he acquired a passion for helping clients through difficult situations and unfortunate circumstances.

Mark earned his Juris Doctor from the University of Missouri-Kansas City School of Law. Throughout law school, Mark gained extensive experience from his internship as an assistant municipal prosecutor working closely with police investigation and trying cases under Missouri Rule 13 Certification. He also worked in criminal defense, personal injury, and local government during his internship.

After law school, Mark worked as a trial lawyer for the Missouri Department of Revenue General Counsel's Office. There, he prosecuted numerous DWI and license restriction cases in St. Louis County and other various counties throughout Missouri. This provided him a distinct insight and expertise into procedure, probable cause, arrests, etc. Mark then moved into the private sector where he could work in all areas of law and trial court. This also allowed him to counsel more and better help those in need.

As a St. Louis native, Mark went to CBC High School and graduated from Mizzou where he earned his Bachelor of Science in Business Administration, Finance and Banking with an emphasis in Economics. Outside of the office, Mark enjoys golfing, running, going out with friends, spending time by the pool with family, and walking his dog Frankie to a local café or park.

Publications: Co-Authored Chapter 14 "Police Departments" of the 2016 Fourth Edition Missouri Local Government Law Deskbook.

Licensed in: Missouri

Ambry Emanuel

Attorney

Ambry Nichole Emanuel earned her Juris Doctor from Saint Louis University School of Law and is a practicing member of the Missouri State Bar. Ambry also holds a Bachelor of Arts in Communications and a Bachelor of Arts in Psychology from Maryville University in St. Louis, where she graduated cum laude.

Ambry has been recognized as a Super Lawyer multiple times, a rating service of outstanding lawyers who have attained a high degree of peer recognition and professional achievement.

While attending law school, Ambry completed three internships at the St. Louis County Prosecuting Attorney’s Office and two internships working in family law. Upon graduation from law school, Ambry worked as an assistant prosecutor, attaining verdicts for the State of Missouri in fifteen out of sixteen trials, the majority of which were domestic violence cases. Ambry then went on to practice family law for five years, and currently practices personal injury and civil litigation law.

Ambry previously worked as a Court Appointed Special Advocate for a child in an abuse and neglect case and has served as a guest speaker at the University of Missouri-St. Louis for an undergraduate law course.
